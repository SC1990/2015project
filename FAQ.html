  • Over 17 million people die from cardiovascular related diseases every year.





  • Smoking, lack of exercise and a poor diet remain some of the leading causes of heart disease. This leads to the narrowing and thickening of the arteries, caused by the build up of cholesterol and other fatty material. The rupture of these can cause a heart attack.





  • Usually, a doctor will conduct a examination called an electrocardiogram, measuring, among others, the electrical activity of your heart and the rate and regularity of your heartbeats.





  • Smoking causes damage to the lining of the blood vessels, increases blood clotting and increases the build-up of fatty deposits in the arteries. Nicotine raises blood pressure and accelerates your heart rate.





  • Chest pain, a fast or irregular heart beat or discomfort radiating to the back, jaw, throat, or arm.





  • To reduce your risk you should: Eat leaner sources of protein such as chicken and fish, Limit cholesterol intake and Increase your intake of fruit and vegetables
